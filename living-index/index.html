Cascades of Refinement

Album

TBA

+

This work arrived initially out of necessity. The original task was to generate organic expression while quarantined with access only to digital tools, but evolved into a dialogue between organic and digital, not as opposites, but as mereologically cohabiting counterparts with equal expressivity. I wanted to bring out and emphasize the subtle artifacts of the digital just as much as the incidental sounds of the acoustic. My goal was not to juxtapose the mercurial and precise, the singular and the infinitely reproductible, but to explore the indefinite space that lies between them as parts of the same composite whole -- a space that continues to collapse and become boundless as technology eliminates the artificial boundary between the two.

Gesturally Articulated Sound

Performance

TBA

+

This project aims to utilize the natural motions, positions, and gestures of the body while playing the violin to control and manipulate parameters within an array of signal processing modules within Max/MSP/Jitter and/or Ableton Live that control sound and visuals. This process works by first attaching an iPhone to the bow arm of the performer. Constant OSC messages of the gyroscope, accelerometer, rotation matrix, and quaternion data of the iPhone are sent via Wi-Fi and interpretted to manipulate parameters within Max/MSP/Jitter and/or Ableton Live in real time, allowing for the boundary between performer, instrument, and technology to be diffused and allow new possibilities of organic expressivity with signal processing. For use within performance and music recording.

Learning Effect is Demonstrated in Vision-Altering Prism Goggles

Research

2022

+

Research performed at University of California, Davis for completion of B.Sc. degree in Neurobiology, Physiology, & Behavior.

Conditions such as right-hemisphere stroke lead to spatial neglect and a reduced ability to complete visuomotor tasks. Prism lens therapy has been shown to be effective as a treatment for visual conditions such as strabismus, as well as efficacy in post-stroke therapy. Here we examined the learning effect present in completion of a visuomotor task while wearing vision-altering prism goggles to better understand the way in which the brain can change its forward model and adapt to altered vision. The hypothesis that the time it takes to complete a simple visuomotor task will decrease the longer the subject wears the vision-altering prism goggles was tested. 10 subjects were given a visuomotor task while wearing one pair of goggles that skew 20° to the left and subsequently one pair that skew 20° to the right, as well as pre- and post-trial controls without goggles. Results supported this hypothesis and demonstrated a potential motor learning effect in addition to the visual learning effect.

Modeling Huntington's Disease in 3-D Striatal Organoids

Research

2021

+

Research conducted at the Buck Institute for Research on Aging in the lab of Dr. Lisa Ellerby on developing relevant three-dimensional striatal organoids for use in modeling Huntington's Disease neuropathogensis.

Techniques used: Human induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC) culturing - maintenance and differentation of iPSC-derived nural stem cells, medium spiny neurons, astrocytes, pericytes & endothelial cells - immunohistochemical and immunocytochemical staining - RNA-seq analysis - high-content digital microscopy and imaging with the Biotek Cytation 5.

Research presented at: University of California, San Francisco (2022), National Advancing Diversity in Aging Research Summit (2021), The Buck Institute for Research on Aging (2021), University of California, Davis Undergraduate Research Conference (2021).

Hologram Plaza

Album

2013

+

Hologram Plaza explores the use of corporate muzak, as a discarded byproduct of industrial production, within the context of accelerationism, liminality, plunderphonics, simulacra, and décollage. The goal of the work is to utilize impersonal musical refuse as a medium to evoke lived human experience and critique the manner in which art is used to manipulate sentiments within public spaces.

Released on Tinnitus Records (cassette), Classic Wave Records (vinyl, cassette), Deluxe Vaporwave Cassettes (cassette), and digital. Due to be re-released by Mental Groove and WRWTFWW Records in 2023. Used in film Lace Crater (2015), discussed in TED Talk with Dan Bell (2017).
