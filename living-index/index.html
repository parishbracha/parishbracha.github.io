Cascades of Refinement

Album

2023

+

This work arrived initially out of necessity. The original task was to generate organic expression while quarantined with access only to digital tools, but evolved into a dialogue between organic and digital, not as opposites, but as mereologically cohabiting counterparts with equal expressivity. I wanted to bring out and emphasize the subtle artifacts of the digital just as much as the incidental sounds of the acoustic. My goal was not to juxtapose the mercurial and precise, the singular and the infinitely reproducible, but to explore the indefinite space that lies between them as parts of the same composite whole -- a space that continues to collapse and become boundless as technology eliminates the artificial boundary between the two.

Gesticulator

Max/MSP Device

2022

+

Gesticulator is a Max4Live device developed to interface with a smartphone to allow for the use of natural and un-conscious body movements to manipulate audio, video, and data signals in real time and through complex customizable parameters. Gesticulator can utilize up to 28 parameters from 15 discrete signals to control any program capable of receiving OSC messages, including Max/MSP and common DAWs like Ableton Live. This device allows its user to utilize their organic motion, gestures, and movement as an interface with digital signal processing and develop a reciprocal and symbiotic relationship between organic body and digital signal that models the relationship between musician and instrument that is seen in many acoustic instruments such as the violin. This device was built with a corresponding performance for violin and smartphone, called Blindsight, in mind to model this relationship. Strapped to the bow arm while playing the violin, the smartphone transmits the speed, position, acceleration, and direction as the performer plays and moves around the performance space and uses these movements to manipulate and process the signals coming from the violin in real time.

Learning Effect is Demonstrated in Visuomotor Tasks with Spatial Neglect

Research

2022

+

Research performed at University of California, Davis for completion of B.Sc. degree in Neurobiology, Physiology, & Behavior with specialization in Neurobiology.

Conditions such as right-hemisphere stroke lead to spatial neglect and a reduced ability to complete visuomotor tasks. Prism lens therapy has been shown to be effective as a treatment for visual conditions such as strabismus, as well as efficacy in post-stroke therapy. Here we examined the learning effect present in completion of a visuomotor task while wearing vision-altering prism goggles to better understand the way in which the brain can change its forward model and adapt to altered vision. The hypothesis that the time it takes to complete a simple visuomotor task will decrease the longer the subject wears the vision-altering prism goggles was tested. 10 subjects were given a visuomotor task while wearing one pair of goggles that skew 20° to the left and subsequently one pair that skew 20° to the right, as well as pre- and post-trial controls without goggles. Results supported this hypothesis and demonstrated a potential motor learning effect in addition to the visual learning effect.

Modeling Huntington's Disease in 3-D Striatal Organoids

Research

2021

+

Research conducted at the Buck Institute for Research on Aging in the lab of Dr. Lisa Ellerby on developing relevant three-dimensional striatal organoids for use in modeling Huntington's Disease neuropathogensis.

Techniques used: Human induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC) culturing - maintenance and differentiation of iPSC-derived neural stem cells, medium spiny neurons, astrocytes, pericytes & endothelial cells - immunohistochemical and immunocytochemical staining - RNA-seq analysis - high-content digital microscopy and imaging with the Biotek Cytation 5.

Research presented at: Yale School of Medicine, University of California, San Francisco (2022), National Advancing Diversity in Aging Research Summit (2021), The Buck Institute for Research on Aging (2021), University of California, Davis Undergraduate Research Conference (2021).

Hologram Plaza

Album

2013

+

Hologram Plaza explores the use of corporate muzak, as a discarded byproduct of industrial production, within the context of accelerationism, liminality, plunderphonics, simulacra, and décollage. The goal of the work is to utilize impersonal musical refuse as a medium to evoke lived human experience and critique the manner in which art is used to manipulate sentiments within public spaces.

Released on Tinnitus Records (cassette), Classic Wave Records (vinyl, cassette), Deluxe Vaporwave Cassettes (cassette), and digital. Due to be re-released by Mental Groove and WRWTFWW Records in 2023. Used in film Lace Crater (2015), discussed in TED Talk with Dan Bell (2017).
